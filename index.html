Mission Statement

"Our non-profit mission reflects a deep desire to protect and restore the natural forests of the Pacific Northwest by combating the spread of invasive English Ivy. We work to remove harmful species, replant native vegetation, and rehabilitate damaged habitats to ensure the resilience of local ecosystems. Through community engagement, education, and hands-on action, we strive to preserve native biodiversity and safeguard the region’s forests for future generations to enjoy and respect."

Invasive Species

English ivy and other invasive plants are rapidly consuming environments throughout the Pacific Northwest. These species smother native vegetation, weaken trees by climbing and adding weight, and disrupt the natural balance of forest ecosystems. Left unchecked, they reduce native biodiversity and sap forests of vital nutrients leaving them vulnerable to disease and rot. Removing invasive plants is essential to protect native habitats, restore ecological health, and ensure resilient forestry.
